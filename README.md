## What am I building? Who is it for?

I am building a CPA study planner and goal-setting tool designed to help users organize and structure their study schedule efficiently. The primary user is myself as I prepare for the CPA exam, but it can also be useful for other students studying for structured, high-stakes exams.

The tool will allow users to input key information such as their test date, number of modules or topics, and estimated hours needed for practice exams. Based on this, it will generate a structured study plan.

## Why? What problem does it solve?

Preparing for the CPA exam requires strong time management and long-term planning. Without a clear structure, it is easy to fall behind or underestimate the amount of time needed to cover all materials.

## This tool solves that problem by:

Breaking down study content into manageable daily or weekly tasks
Distributing workload based on time remaining until the exam
Ensuring enough time is allocated for review and practice exams
Reducing stress by providing a clear, organized plan

Instead of manually planning in spreadsheets or notes, this tool automates the process and creates a more reliable and consistent study schedule.

## MVP vs. Stretch Goals
MVP (Minimum Viable Product)
User inputs:
Test date
Number of modules/topics
Estimated total study hours
Practice exam time
Automatically generate a study schedule (daily or weekly breakdown)
Simple UI with input fields and generated plan display
Ability to adjust inputs and regenerate the plan
Stretch Goals
Calendar view (visual schedule layout)
Progress tracking (mark modules as completed)
Adaptive scheduling (recalculate plan if user falls behind)
